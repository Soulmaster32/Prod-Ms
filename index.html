🏭 Taganito HPAL Nickel Corporation (THPAL) Profile

THPAL is a pioneering hydrometallurgical processing plant situated at the Taganito Special Economic Zone in Claver, Surigao del Norte. Utilizing advanced High-Pressure Acid Leach (HPAL) technology, the plant extracts high-value nickel and cobalt from low-grade lateritic/limonite ore.

⚡ STRATEGIC REFINING VALUE CHAIN

Low-grade ore (1.15% - 1.35% Ni) supplied by Taganito Mining Corporation (TMC) is processed in high-temperature titanium autoclaves using sulfuric acid ($H_2SO_4$). The resulting Nickel/Cobalt Mixed Sulfide is exported directly to Sumitomo Metal Mining's (SMM) Niihama Nickel Refinery in Japan to produce 99.99% electrolytic battery-grade nickel.

Ore Preparation & Slurry Screening

Low-grade limonite ore delivered by Taganito Mining Corp (TMC) conveyor systems is screened, washed, and mixed with water in ball mills to produce a uniform slurry with optimal solid density (~35-40%) ready for autoclave feeding.

Complex Overview

Panoramic View of Taganito Hydrometallurgical Complex

Located at Barangay Taganito, Claver, Surigao del Norte. The 30,000 MT/year capacity plant operates inside the Taganito Special Economic Zone as a registered Ecozone Export Enterprise.

Core Technology

High-Pressure Acid Leach (HPAL) Autoclave Systems

Titanium-lined autoclaves operate at high temperature (approx. 250-260°C) and pressure (4.5 MPa), leaching nickel and cobalt selectively with sulfuric acid while rejecting iron, aluminum, and silicon.

DCS Automation

Centralized Control Room & Automated Assurance

24/7 real-time telemetry monitoring valve isolation, $H_2S$ gas reactivity, and automated neutralization sequences to ensure highest extraction recovery rates and operator safety.

Stage 03 Separation

Countercurrent Decantation (CCD) Thickeners

Massive multi-stage decantation circuits separate the nickel/cobalt pregnant leach solution (PLS) from the barren residue solids through continuous countercurrent washing.

Environmental Care

Sencio Creek Clean-Up & Water Quality Preservation

In partnership with EMB Region XIII and Barangay Hayanggabon residents, THPAL actively adopts and preserves local water bodies, conducting regular orchestrated coastal and creek clean-up drives.

🌿 Environmental Compliance & MMT Protocol

THPAL operates under strict compliance with DENR-EMB Region 13 regulations. Regular monitoring is conducted by the Multi-Partite Monitoring Team (MMT) representing local LGUs, indigenous communities, and environmental regulators.

♻️ Tailings & By-Product Innovation

• TSF1 Facility Management: Tailings consisting predominantly of inert hematite and gypsum are neutralized and safely deposited in engineered Tailings Storage Facility 1 (THPAL TSF1).
• Scandium Recovery Project: Innovating beyond nickel, THPAL utilizes barren liquor from the HPAL process to extract valuable Scandium Oxalate (~23% Sc) using resin and oxalic acid, turning industrial waste into high-tech commercial resources.

🤝 Community & Ecological Adoption Active adopter of a 1.6 km stretch of Sencio Creek and coastal zones in Brgy. Hayanggabon and Taganito, engaging local communities in continuous environmental education and preservation.
